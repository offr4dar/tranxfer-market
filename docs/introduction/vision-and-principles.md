# Vision & Principles

## Vision

To become the definitive digital platform for grassroots and academy football recruitment — giving every talented player, regardless of geography or circumstance, a fair chance to be discovered.

---

## Core Principles

These principles were established during early product design and should inform every feature decision made on the platform.

### 1. Scout-Led, Not Player-Initiated

Football recruitment is culturally scout-led. Players are discovered — they do not apply. Any feature that inverts this dynamic risks feeling unnatural to both players and scouts, and could undermine the platform's credibility with the professional scouting community.

Players on Tranxfer Market build their presence and wait to be found. Scouts do the finding. This is not a limitation — it is a deliberate design choice that respects the culture of the sport.

### 2. Authenticity Over Vanity

Stats on a player card mean nothing if they are self-reported and unverifiable. The platform is designed around verifiable, scout-endorsed ratings. A scout's name sits behind every endorsement they make. This creates accountability and makes each endorsement genuinely meaningful.

### 3. Scouts Are the Primary Engine

Freelance scouts are the most naturally engaged user type on the platform because it directly serves their livelihood. Features that make scouts more effective also drive player discovery, platform credibility, and organic growth. When in doubt, optimise for the scout experience.

### 4. Grassroots Reality

Grassroots football does not have match statistics, player databases, or performance data. The platform must work entirely with what is available — video, self-reported attributes, scout endorsements, and in-app activity. Features that assume real-world data exist will not work for this audience.

### 5. Organic Growth Through Value

The best growth mechanism on Tranxfer Market is a player or scout experiencing real value and telling someone about it. Features should be designed to create those moments — a player sharing their card, a scout endorsing a player they believe in, a connection forming that leads somewhere real.

---

## What Tranxfer Market Is Not

- It is not a social network. Engagement features exist to serve recruitment, not replace it.
- It is not a job board. Players do not post "available" listings and wait for responses.
- It is not a stats platform. Without real match data, statistical performance tracking is not viable at this level.
- It is not trying to replace scouts. It is trying to make them better at their job.
