# The Endorsement Growth Loop

The endorsement system is the most strategically important mechanic on the platform. It is simultaneously an engagement feature, a credibility system, and an organic acquisition loop.

---

## The Loop

```
Player wants Scout Pick or Elite card tier
        ↓
Needs a scout endorsement to qualify
        ↓
Player contacts their real-world scout
and asks them to join Tranxfer Market
        ↓
Scout joins the platform to endorse the player
        ↓
Scout rates the player across six attributes
        ↓
Player card upgrades — Scout Verified badge appears
Scout's name is publicly credited on the card
        ↓
Scout discovers platform value independently:
watchlists, discovery tools, portfolio
        ↓
Scout begins using the platform for their own work
        ↓
Scout brings additional players to their attention
        ↓
Platform grows on both sides simultaneously
```

---

## Why This Works

### For the Player
The incentive is direct and personal — a better card. The Scout Pick tier requires a watchlist save, but the Scout Verified badge (and the credibility it carries) requires a real endorsement from a named scout. Players care about this because:

- It makes their card mean something verifiable
- It is a status signal to friends and peers
- It represents real external validation of their ability

### For the Scout
Joining to endorse a specific player is a low-friction entry point. Once they are on the platform, the value proposition reveals itself naturally. Critically:

- Their name is publicly credited on the player's card — this is professional recognition
- Every card share is a public attribution: *Scout Verified · J. Hargreaves* — their reputation travels with the player
- If the player goes on to do well, the scout's early endorsement becomes a valuable part of their track record

### For the Platform
Each endorsement creates a permanent, attributable link between two real people. This is not follower-following social graph data — it is professional credibility data. The platform becomes more valuable with every endorsement added because:

- The scout's presence attracts other players they know
- The endorsed player's card becomes a distribution channel for the platform
- The trust graph that emerges from the endorsement network is a genuine competitive moat

---

## Key Design Decisions That Enable the Loop

### Scout Retains Full Control
The scout decides whether to endorse. They are never automatically credited. This means:
- Scouts are selective — only players they genuinely believe in get endorsed
- Each endorsement carries weight precisely because it is not automatic
- Scouts protect their own reputation by being discerning

### Revocation Is Possible
Scouts can revoke an endorsement. This further reinforces that endorsements are real professional acts with real stakes — not throwaway social likes.

### Attribution Is Permanent and Visible
The scout's name on the card is not hidden in a settings menu. It is displayed prominently. This makes endorsement a visible professional credential for the scout, not just a service done for the player.

### The Card Tier Is the Incentive Bridge
Without the card tier system, players would have less motivation to seek endorsements. The Scout Pick tier (brand green card) is visually distinct enough to be worth pursuing. Players will actively recruit scouts to get it.

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| Endorsement request rate | How many players are actively pursuing endorsements |
| Endorsement acceptance rate | Scout quality and engagement; low rate may indicate friction in the flow |
| Scout activation rate post-endorsement | What % of scouts who join to endorse go on to use other features |
| Cards shared after tier upgrade | Whether the card tier is genuinely motivating sharing behaviour |
| Scout-referred player signups | How many players join because a scout mentioned the platform |
