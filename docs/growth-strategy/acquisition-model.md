# Acquisition Model

---

## The Core Challenge

Tranxfer Market operates in a market where neither side has value without the other. Players need scouts on the platform to give their profiles meaning. Scouts need a sufficient volume of quality player profiles to make the platform worth using. This is the classic two-sided marketplace chicken-and-egg problem.

The platform's approach to solving this is to **launch player and scout-first**, avoiding the complexity of the club side until there is sufficient supply and demand on the first two sides.

---

## Primary Acquisition Channels

### 1. Organic — Player Card Sharing

Every time a player shares their card to WhatsApp, Instagram Stories, or X, the Tranxfer Market watermark travels with it. For a target audience of 14–21 year olds deeply embedded in social media culture, a visually compelling shareable card has genuine viral potential.

The card tier system amplifies this — players want to show off a Scout Pick or Elite card, and those only come from real platform engagement. Sharing is not vanity; it is a signal of achievement.

**Estimated reach per share:** A player sharing to their WhatsApp circle or Instagram story likely reaches 50–200 peers in the same demographic — the exact target audience.

### 2. Organic — The Endorsement Loop

The endorsement mechanic is the most powerful acquisition driver on the platform because it turns players into a direct sales channel for scout acquisition.

See [The Endorsement Growth Loop](endorsement-growth-loop.md) for the full breakdown.

### 3. Freelance Scout Networks

Freelance scouts are the highest-leverage early adopter segment. A single active freelance scout may know dozens of players across multiple teams and age groups. If the platform genuinely improves their workflow, they will advocate for it within their network — bringing both players and other scouts.

Target the freelance scout community first, before club-hired scouts, because their personal incentive to use the platform is strongest.

### 4. Grassroots Football Communities

Grassroots football has a rich ecosystem of online communities — Facebook groups for local leagues, Discord servers, Reddit communities, YouTube channels focused on amateur football. These are low-cost, high-relevance channels.

Content that will resonate: the player card visual (shareable, football-native aesthetic), the endorsement mechanic story, and the scout discovery angle.

---

## Launch Sequencing

| Phase | Focus | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-launch | Build player supply | Scouts need profiles to browse — launch with content |
| Launch | Players + freelance scouts | Highest engagement on both sides; endorsement loop activates |
| Post-launch | Club-hired scouts | Once player volume is established, club scouts have reason to join |
| Expansion | Clubs and teams | Two-sided marketplace unlocked once scout and player sides are proven |

---

## North Star Metric

**Scout-to-player connections made** — the number of meaningful interactions between scouts and players that would not have happened without the platform. This captures the core value proposition better than DAU or profile count alone.
