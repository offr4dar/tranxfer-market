# User Types & Personas

Tranxfer Market has three user roles built into the data model. At launch, the focus is on players and scouts. Clubs are modelled but not a primary acquisition target initially.

---

## Players

### Who They Are
Grassroots and academy players, typically aged 14–21, looking to increase their visibility and be discovered by scouts or clubs. They may be playing for a Sunday league team, a grassroots academy, or a school team. They are unlikely to have any professional-level exposure.

### What They Want
- To be seen by the right people
- To have a professional-looking profile they can share
- To know whether scouts are noticing them
- Validation that they are good enough to be discovered

### Motivations & Behaviours
- Highly motivated to complete their profile if they believe it will lead to discovery
- Responsive to signals of scout interest — even anonymised view counts drive return visits
- Social sharing instinct is high — a shareable player card is a natural fit
- Likely to recruit their own scouts and coaches to the platform if there is a clear personal benefit (e.g. endorsement unlocking a higher card tier)

### Key Engagement Drivers
- Scout interest signal (anonymised profile views)
- Player card tier progression
- Profile completion gamification
- Weekly challenges with video submission

---

## Scouts — Club-Hired

### Who They Are
Scouts employed by a football club, typically tasked with identifying talent within a specific region, age group, or position. They report to a head of recruitment or academy director and need to present shortlists in a structured, shareable format.

### What They Want
- Efficiency — the ability to filter, find, and evaluate players quickly
- Tools to organise and present their findings to the club
- A reliable source of players they might not have found through their existing network

### Motivations & Behaviours
- Engagement is task-driven rather than habitual — they come to the platform with a specific brief
- Value tools that reduce admin: saved searches, tagged watchlists, shareable shortlists
- Reputation within the club depends on the quality of their recommendations, so credibility of platform data matters

### Key Engagement Drivers
- Smart filters and saved searches
- Watchlists with tagging and notes
- Shareable shortlist reports

---

## Scouts — Freelance

### Who They Are
Independent scouts who identify talent and present it to clubs on a commission or retainer basis. Their livelihood depends on finding players before anyone else does and having the credibility to convince clubs to act on their recommendations.

### What They Want
- A competitive edge in finding unnoticed talent
- A way to build and demonstrate their track record
- Credibility signals that make their recommendations carry weight

### Motivations & Behaviours
- **The highest-engagement user type on the platform.** The platform directly serves their livelihood, so they are motivated to use it obsessively.
- Naturally incentivised to bring players onto the platform — more profiles means more talent to discover
- Public endorsements are a reputational act for them, not just for the player — they will be selective, which makes endorsements credible
- Their placement history (players recommended who went on to sign) is their professional portfolio

### Key Engagement Drivers
- Talent discovery tools with exclusivity feel
- Freelance portfolio / placement track record
- Scout endorsement system — their name on a player's card is a credibility signal for both parties
- Platform-driven acquisition: players invite scouts to endorse them, scouts discover the platform's value

---

## Clubs & Teams

### Who They Are
Amateur, semi-professional, or lower-league clubs looking to recruit players or post trial opportunities.

### Current Status
Clubs are included in the data model and onboarding flow but are not a primary focus at launch. The chicken-and-egg problem of a two-sided marketplace means the platform will launch player and scout-first, then bring clubs in once there is sufficient player supply to make the platform valuable to them.

### Future Potential
- Trial day and open-slot posting
- Direct messaging with connected players
- Club profile pages with squad information
