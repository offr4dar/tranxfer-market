# Scout Qualifications, FA Partnership Strategy & Dev Instructions

## Part 1 — Discussion Summary

### The Landscape: Who Does What

Nobody currently builds the thing Tranxfer Market is proposing — a platform where a scout's profile aggregates their DBS status, their FA Talent ID qualifications, their PFSA certifications, their club affiliations, their endorsements from players and other scouts, and their real activity history, all in one verified, portable identity.

Here is what currently exists and what each provider actually covers:

#### The FA — Governing Body

The FA runs its own Talent Identification pathway (Levels 1–5) but does **not** require any of it for someone to operate as a scout. There is no FA scout registration system — only agent registration exists formally. At grassroots level, the FA's safeguarding guidance for scouts amounts to: "challenge unidentified adults at matches and ask to see club-issued ID."

The FA uses **First Advantage Europe Limited** as its DBS check partner for processing criminal record checks on individuals in roles that interact with children.

The FA does **not** have a safeguarding data partner in the sense Tranxfer Market is proposing. They have reactive case management and DBS outsourcing, but no centralised, live registry of who is scouting at grassroots level.

#### The PFSA — Private Training & Credentialing Body

The Professional Football Scouts Association is a not-for-profit founded in 2013. It is **not FA-recognised** — it is entirely self-accrediting. Their courses are CPD accredited (a generic continuing professional development framework). They have 25,000+ members and partnerships with clubs from the Championship to National League.

What their membership gives you:
- A Digital Scout ID with QR code
- 7 analysis tools (Tactical Board, Image Telestration, etc.)
- DBS check discounts (via Personnel Checks)
- Blue-tick verification on PFSA profiles (for certified members)
- Access to the PFSA Scouting Network (assignment-based report writing for clubs)

What their membership does **not** give you:
- Independent DBS verification
- Aggregation of external credentials (FA courses, club IDs)
- Endorsements from players or behavioural tracking
- A two-sided marketplace connecting scouts and players

The PFSA verifies that you are PFSA-certified. It is self-referential.

#### IPSO — International Professional Scouting Organisation

A smaller private education provider offering scouting courses endorsed by the CPD Group and several overseas FAs (Romania, Uzbekistan, India, Estonia). Not FA-recognised in England. Runs Level 1–3 courses and workshops focused on talent identification and match analysis.

#### InScout Network — Marketplace (Early Stage)

The closest thing to a competitor marketplace. Verifies credentials (FIFA agent licences, club email addresses) before granting access. However, still in early development — operating primarily through a LinkedIn hub, planning to move to an app. Verification is a manual one-time gate at sign-up with no ongoing behavioural data, no endorsement mechanics, and no aggregation of external qualifications.

#### Scout'd — Player Showcase Platform

UK-based, focused on players below the professional game building digital profiles for AI-powered discovery. Not about scout verification.

#### UEFA Elite Scout Programme

High-end programme (€7,900) for professional scouts. Requires 2+ years' experience in football. Delivered every two years with a maximum of 35 participants. Relevant as a credential scouts may hold but not a competing platform.

### The Gap Tranxfer Market Fills

The current state: a scout carries their DBS certificate, their FA Level 2, their PFSA certification, and their club ID card all separately — if they carry them at all. None of these credentials talk to each other. There is no platform that:

1. Aggregates all credentials in one verified profile
2. Independently validates DBS status
3. Tracks ongoing behavioural reputation through real interactions
4. Creates a two-sided demand loop where players need verified scouts and scouts need to be where the players are

The PFSA could theoretically pivot toward this, but their model is training-and-certification, not marketplace. InScout has marketplace architecture but is early stage and lacks the endorsement growth mechanic. Nobody else is even close.

### FA Partnership Strategy

**Pre-launch (now → working MVP):** Approach the FA Safeguarding team (not Talent ID) for an informal advisory conversation. Frame: "We're building infrastructure that addresses the gap in scout verification at grassroots level. We want to make sure our safeguarding framework meets the standards you'd expect." This is a consultation, not an endorsement ask.

**What to demo:** The verification flow working end-to-end on even a single scout — how DBS is validated, how FA and PFSA credentials are linked, how endorsements work, what the verified profile looks like, and what data is captured.

**Post-traction:** Convert the advisory relationship into a formal partnership once there are enough verified scouts to make the data meaningful (a few hundred with real activity history). The pitch becomes: "We've built something that aligns with your regulatory objectives. Let's talk interoperability."

**Entry point:** FA Safeguarding team. Safeguarding is the word that opens doors fastest. The Talent ID team will see a tech platform and not know what to do with it.

## Part 2 — UK Scout Qualifications Reference

The following qualifications are recognised within UK football scouting. None are mandatory to operate as a scout — the industry has no licensing requirement — but they represent the credentials scouts may hold and should be able to display on their Tranxfer Market profile.

### FA Talent Identification Pathway (Official — The FA)

| Level | Name | Format | Prerequisites | Target Audience |
|-------|------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Level 1 | Introduction to Talent Identification | Free, online, self-paced | None | Anyone interested in scouting |
| Level 2 | National Talent Identification & Scouting in Football | 2-day course | FA Level 1 + valid FA Safeguarding Children certificate | Scouts working in or aspiring to professional game |
| Level 3 | Advanced Principles of Talent Identification | 12 days (6×2-day modules) over 10 months | FA Level 2 + valid safeguarding + appropriate job role in professional football | Full-time scouts and talent ID staff |
| Level 4 | Talent Management, Strategy & Leadership | 19 days over 14 months | FA Level 3 + senior scouting/recruitment role | Heads of recruitment, senior scouting staff |
| Level 5 | Technical Directors Programme | 25 days over 20 months | FA Level 4 + technical director role or identified potential | Current or aspiring technical directors |

### FA Safeguarding Courses (Official — The FA)

| Course | Format | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Safeguarding Children Workshop | Online or in-person | Required prerequisite for FA Talent ID Level 2+ |

## Part 3 — Dev Instructions: Scout Qualifications Section

### Overview

Add a **Qualifications** section to the scout profile that allows scouts to list and display their scouting-related certifications. This sits on the scout's public profile and is separate from the verification checklist (which handles DBS and identity verification). Qualifications are self-declared with optional evidence upload — the platform displays them but does not verify course completion at this stage.

This section should be built as part of the scout onboarding flow (after the verification checklist is complete) and also accessible via the profile edit screen.

### UI Components to Build

#### 1. QualificationsSection (Profile Display)

Location: `components/QualificationsSection.tsx`

Renders on the scout's public profile. Shows a list of qualifications grouped by provider with provider logos/icons. Each item shows:
- Provider badge (FA shield, PFSA logo, etc. — use coloured initials as fallback)
- Qualification name and level
- Year completed (if provided)
- Verified badge if certificate has been uploaded (small checkmark icon)

If the viewing user is the profile owner, show an "Add Qualification" button at the bottom.

Empty state: "No qualifications added yet" with an "Add your first qualification" CTA.

#### 2. AddQualificationSheet (Slide-up Panel)

Location: `components/AddQualificationSheet.tsx`

Uses the same slide-up panel pattern as `PerformanceLogSheet.tsx`. Flow:

**Step 1 — Choose provider:** Grid of provider options with icons:
- The FA (shield icon)
- PFSA
- IPSO
- UEFA
- S4 Scouting
- Other (custom entry)

**Step 2 — Choose qualification:** Filtered list from `qualification_catalogue` for the selected provider. Each item shows name, level, and short description. If provider is "Other", show free-text fields for name and provider.

**Step 3 — Details:** 
- Year completed (year picker, optional)
- Upload certificate image (optional, uses Supabase Storage)
- Save button

The sheet should animate between steps with the same parallax pattern used in the onboarding wizard.
